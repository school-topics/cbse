## What this audit tells you (Coordinates chapter)

**Your app covers the conceptual core well:** axes, origin, quadrants, distance
formula, reflection. Those are the [covered] rows and they're the right
foundation.

**The book goes deeper in six specific places** — these were the [gap] rows
that aren't "drag a point". **All six are now built as deep-dive interactives**
(reachable from the home menu → Deep Dives):
1. Collinearity without plotting (C1-3.3) ✅
2. Midpoint derivation + trisection (C1-3.5) ✅
3. Points on a circle / distance from origin (C1-3.6) ✅
4. Reconstruct vertices from midpoints (C1-3.7) ✅
5. City street-grid modelling (C1-3.8) ✅
6. Pixel-screen modelling (C1-3.9) ✅

All six share one grid/axis helper and one pointer pipeline (no duplicated
axis-rendering), and each is tagged `data-ncert="<ID>"` for later book↔activity
linking. The question bank was extended so the deeper material (circle,
find-the-endpoint, reverse-midpoints, ordered-pair) also shows up in
Practice/Quiz/Challenge, not only in deep-dives.

**Coverage score, updated (validated against the textbook PDF):**
**13 covered, 9 partial, 0 gap, 1 text** (23 rows).
The six deep-dive gaps and the (x,y)≠(y,x) gap (C1-1.4b) are closed. The one
remaining not-fully-covered item is C1-3.4 (book Q8), an open-ended free-triangle
construction consciously deferred with a note above. Remaining [partial] rows are

reflection, C1-3.1 RAMP, C1-3.10 square-check) — all touched by at least one

against the NCERT chapter (iemh101.pdf) and matches exactly — Q6/Q7 collinearity
triples, the Q9 midpoint table, Q10 reverse-endpoint (−17,6), Q11 trisection

## How to reuse this as a template (for the other chapters)

1. Copy this file, rename per chapter (e.g. `numbers-coverage-map.md`).
2. Walk the chapter's section headings (X.1, X.2, subsections) top to bottom —
   the book's own contents *is* the checklist. One row each.
3. Fill `ID` as `C{chapter}-{group}.{n}` so every unit has a stable anchor.
4. Fill "What the book does" from the section; keep it to one line.
5. Mark `App status` honestly against what's actually built.
6. The [gap] and [partial] rows become your build backlog for that chapter.
