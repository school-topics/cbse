# Deep-dive spec — Points on a circle / distance from origin (C1-3.6)

**Coverage-map ID:** `C1-3.6` (End-of-Chapter Q12)
**Tier:** medium (reuses live-distance pattern from Explore)
**Concept:** the distance of a point from the origin is its "radius"; points at
equal distance from O lie on a circle; classify inside / on / outside.

## Why this exists
Book Q12: show three points lie on a circle centred at O; find the radius; then
test whether other points are inside, on, or outside. Turns the distance formula
into a geometric classifier.

## Behaviour (must-haves)
1. Coordinate plane with origin O, axes, light grid.
2. A circle centred at O with an adjustable radius (slider), drawn live.
3. Draggable test point P. Live readout: |OP| = √(x²+y²), and a verdict:
   inside (|OP| < r), on (|OP| = r, within a small tolerance), or outside.
4. Colour the verdict (use app tokens: inside/on/outside get distinct but
   accessible colours; keep to two ramps + neutral).

## Nice-to-haves
- Drop several fixed points and let the student set the radius until three of
  them land exactly "on" — rediscovering the book's √65 circle.
- "Load book problem" button: places A(1,−8), B(−4,7), C(−7,−4) and sets r=√65
  so all three read "on"; then D(−5,6) reads inside, E(0,9) reads outside.

## Correctness checklist
- |OP| uses √(x²+y²) (distance from origin specifically).
- "On the circle" needs a tolerance (floating point) — e.g. |(|OP| − r)| < 0.05
  in data units; document the tolerance.
- Book check: |OA|=|OB|=|OC|=√65 ≈ 8.062; D inside (√61≈7.81), E outside (9).
