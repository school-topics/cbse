# Deep-dive spec — Midpoint and trisection (C1-3.5)

**Coverage-map ID:** `C1-3.5` (End-of-Chapter Q9–Q11)
**Tier:** HARD — discovery-based. The goal is NOT to show the midpoint formula;
it's to make the student *notice* the averaging pattern themselves.
**Concept:** the midpoint of ST has coordinates that are the average of S and T;
trisection points divide the segment 1:2 and 2:1.

## Why this exists
Book Q9 gives a table of S, M, T and asks "is M the midpoint?" then "can you find
a connection between the coordinates?" — i.e. it wants the student to *derive*
midpoint = average, not be told it. Q10–Q11 apply it (find missing endpoint;
trisection). The current app lists "midpoint" as a feature but doesn't teach the
discovery.

## Behaviour (must-haves) — DISCOVERY FLOW, in this order
1. Segment with draggable endpoints S and T, and a draggable point M the student
   places where they *think* the midpoint is.
2. Show S, M, T coordinates live. Do NOT show the formula yet.
3. A "check" that tells them only whether M is exactly halfway (yes/no), not the
   answer — so they adjust by feel first.
4. After they get it right (or after N tries), reveal the pattern prompt:
   "Look at S, your M, and T. What is (Sx+Tx)/2? What is (Sy+Ty)/2?" with the
   three numbers shown so the average pattern is visible.
5. Only then, state: midpoint = ((Sx+Tx)/2, (Sy+Ty)/2). Now snap M to the exact
   midpoint and let them drag S,T to see it always hold.

## Nice-to-haves
- Trisection mode: show P (closer to S) and Q (closer to T) dividing ST into
  thirds. P = S + (T−S)/3, Q = S + 2(T−S)/3. Let them predict, then reveal.
- "Reverse" mini-task echoing Q10: fix M and one endpoint, ask for the other
  (endpoint = 2M − known). This previews the harder C1-3.7 deep-dive.

## Pedagogy checklist (the part that needs YOUR review)
- The reveal must come AFTER the student's own attempt, not before. If the
  formula shows immediately, the discovery is lost and this becomes a calculator.
  This ordering is the whole value of the activity — protect it in review.
- "Exactly halfway" needs a tolerance; when checking their placed M, snap-accept
  if within a small distance, and treat that as success.
- Don't over-praise; a simple "that's it — now look at the numbers" is enough.

## Correctness checklist
- Midpoint = component-wise average. Test: S(−3,0),T(3,0) → M(0,0). S(2,3),T(4,5)
  → M(3,4). S(0,0),T(0,−10) → M(0,−5). (These are the book's table rows.)
- Trisection: A(4,7),B(16,−2) → P=(8,4), Q=(12,1). Verify.
- Reverse: M(−7,1), A(3,−4) → B = 2M−A = (−17,6). Verify.
