## PRACTICE (guided, explained)

**P1 — read coordinates off a figure** · `C1-1.3c` · easy
Q: A door runs from D(8,0) to R(11.5,0). How wide is the door?
A: Width = |11.5 − 8| = 3.5 units. Both points on the x-axis, so just subtract
x-coordinates. Explanation hook: is 3.5 ft a comfortable, wheelchair-friendly
door width? (ties to book's accessibility Think-and-Reflect)

**P2 — point on an axis** · `C1-1.3b` · easy
Q: Where does P(0, −4.5) lie?
A: On the y-axis (x=0), 4.5 units below the origin. Sign of y is negative → below O.

**P3 — quadrant identification** · `C1-1.4a` · easy
Q: Which quadrant is (−5, 3) in?
A: Quadrant II. x negative, y positive → (−,+) → QII.

**P4 — complete the rectangle** · `C1-1.4c` · medium
Q: A table's feet are at (8,9), (11,9), (11,7). Where is the fourth foot?
A: (8,7). Opposite corner shares the first point's x and the third point's y.
Width = 3, length = 2.

**P5 — distance parallel to an axis** · `C1-2.1` · easy
Q: Distance from W1(3,0) to W2(7,0)?
A: |7−3| = 4 units. Same y, so horizontal distance is the x-difference.

**P6 — distance formula (diagonal)** · `C1-2.2` · medium
Q: Distance from A(3,4) to D(7,1)?
A: √((7−3)² + (1−4)²) = √(16+9) = √25 = 5 units.

**P7 — reflection in the y-axis** · `C1-2.3` · medium
Q: Reflect M(9,6) in the y-axis. New coordinates? Does length to O change?
A: M'(−9,6). Distance to O unchanged (reflection preserves distance): both √117.

**P8 — reflection in the x-axis** · `C1-2.4` · medium
Q: Reflect D(7,1) in the x-axis.
A: D'(7,−1). x stays, y negates.

**P9 — (x,y) vs (y,x)** · `C1-1.4b` · medium
Q: Is (3,5) the same point as (5,3)?
A: No. Order matters. They coincide only if x=y. (Great to pair with the
city-grid deep-dive, same idea.)

**P10 — midpoint (pattern spotting)** · `C1-3.5` · medium
Q: Is M(0,0) the midpoint of S(−3,0) and T(3,0)?
A: Yes. Midpoint = average of coordinates = ((−3+3)/2, (0+0)/2) = (0,0).
Explanation: this is the averaging pattern the midpoint deep-dive builds on.

## CHALLENGE (progressive, exemplar-level)

### Set 1 — warm up
**C1.1** · `C1-3.1` · Consider R(3,0), A(0,−2), M(−5,−2), P(−5,2) joined in order.
(i) Which two sides are perpendicular? (ii) Which side is parallel to an axis?
(iii) Which two points are mirror images, and in which axis?
Worked: MP is vertical (x=−5 both), AM is horizontal-ish check: A(0,−2),M(−5,−2)
share y → AM parallel to x-axis. MP shares x → parallel to y-axis, and AM⊥MP.
M(−5,−2) and P(−5,2) are mirror images in the x-axis.

**C1.2** · `C1-3.10` · Plot A(2,1), B(−1,2), C(−2,−1), D(1,−2). Is ABCD a square? Area?
Worked: all sides = √10 (e.g. AB = √((−1−2)²+(2−1)²)=√10), diagonals equal →
square. Area = side² = 10 sq units.

### Set 2 — push
**C2.1** · `C1-3.3` · Are M(−3,−4), A(0,0), G(6,8) collinear? Give a method
without plotting.
Worked: slope OA→ from A(0,0) to G(6,8) = 8/6 = 4/3; A(0,0) to M(−3,−4) = −4/−3
= 4/3. Equal slopes through a shared point → collinear. (This is the
collinearity deep-dive's concept.)

**C2.2** · `C1-3.5` · M(−7,1) is the midpoint of A(3,−4) and B(x,y). Find B.
Worked: midpoint = average → −7 = (3+x)/2 → x = −17; 1 = (−4+y)/2 → y = 6.
B = (−17, 6).

### Set 3 — exemplar / reasoning
**C3.1 (True/False with reason)** · `C1-1.4b` · "For any point, (x,y) and (y,x)
are the same point." True or false, justify.
Worked: False. They're equal only when x=y; otherwise distinct (e.g. (3,5)≠(5,3)).

**C3.2** · `C1-3.7` · The midpoints of triangle ABC's sides are D(5,1), E(6,5),
F(0,3). Find A, B, C.
Worked: vertex = sum of two adjacent midpoints minus the opposite one.
A = D+F−E = (5+0−6, 1+3−5) = (−1,−1); B = D+E−F = (11,3); C = E+F−D = (1,7).
(Verify: midpoint of A,B = (5,1)=D ✓.) This is the hardest deep-dive's concept.

**C3.3** · `C1-3.6` · Show A(1,−8), B(−4,7), C(−7,−4) lie on a circle centred at
O. Radius? Do D(−5,6), E(0,9) lie inside/on/outside?
Worked: |OA|=√(1+64)=√65; |OB|=√(16+49)=√65; |OC|=√(49+16)=√65 → all on circle
radius √65. |OD|=√(25+36)=√61 < √65 → inside. |OE|=√(0+81)=9=√81 > √65 → outside.
